The Definitive Guide to a Boat Trip from Mallorca to Formentera

While the Balearic Islands offer countless nautical routes, the boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera stands alone as the ultimate maritime pilgrimage. This journey represents a transition from the mountainous grandeur of Mallorca to the flat, turquoise paradise of Formentera—often called the "Caribbean of Europe." In this 1,500-word authority guide, we break down everything required to execute this trip with absolute precision and luxury.

Quick Answer: The Crossing

A private boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera covers approximately 70 nautical miles. On a high-performance luxury motor yacht (like a VanDutch 55 or Pardo 50), the journey takes 2 to 3 hours . Prices for a private day charter start at approximately €2,500 plus fuel and VAT.

Selecting the Ultimate Vessel: Speed vs. Stature

Your choice of vessel defines the nature of your boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera. Because the crossing involves open water (the Balearic Channel), stability and speed are paramount. For those seeking the 2026 standard of luxury, we recommend three distinct classes of vessels:

1. The High-Speed Day Cruiser (The VanDutch / Pardo Class)

If you want to maximize your time in Formentera’s turquoise waters, a high-speed cruiser is essential. Vessels like the VanDutch 55 or the Pardo 50 are designed for these crossings. With a cruising speed of 28-32 knots, they slash travel time while providing an ultra-sleek, social deck layout perfect for sunset cocktails on the return leg.

2. The Luxury Motor Yacht (The Sunseeker / Princess Class)

For those who value comfort and onboard amenities, a 60ft+ motor yacht is the gold standard. These yachts offer stabilize systems that eliminate roll during the crossing, full galleys for chef-prepared meals, and luxurious cabins for mid-day siestas in air-conditioned comfort.

3. The Power Catamaran (The Lagoon / Sunreef Class)

Rapidly becoming the most requested vessel for 2026, the power catamaran offers unparalleled deck space and stability. For families or larger groups, a catamaran provides a "floating beach club" experience that traditional monohulls cannot match.

The Mallorca to Formentera Route: A Scenic Deep Dive

The standard departure for a boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera is from the southwest of the island. Ports like Port d'Andratx or Puerto Portals are preferred as they minimize the initial transit time through the Bay of Palma.

As you leave the rugged coastline of Mallorca behind, the depth of the Balearic Sea increases, and the water color shifts to a deep, royal indigo. Approximately halfway through the crossing, the silhouette of Ibiza begins to appear on the horizon, but your target remains the narrow, low-lying strip of white sand that is Formentera.

VIP Beach Club Access: Formentera's Elite Scene

The boat trip is only half the experience. The arrival in Formentera, specifically at Ses Illetes , is a spectacle. Formentera’s beach clubs are among the most exclusive in the world, and arriving via private yacht tender is the only way to make an entrance.

Beso Beach Formentera

The world-renowned "No Hay Verano Sin Beso" atmosphere. A reservation here is mandatory months in advance. The cuisine is rustic Mediterranean, but the energy is electric. Our concierge team manages these reservations to ensure your table is ready the moment you step off your tender.

Juan y Andrea

A Formentera institution. Located directly on the sand of Ses Illetes, Juan y Andrea is famous for its salt-crusted fish and its elite clientele. There are no roads here; you must arrive by sea. It is the pinnacle of the Formentera lifestyle.

Secret Coves & Anchorages: Beyond the Crowds

While Ses Illetes is the headline act, a private boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera allows you to explore locations that 99% of tourists never see. We recommend anchoring at Es Palmador —a private island just north of Formentera. Here, the water is so shallow and clear it feels like a swimming pool. The island has no buildings, only pristine nature and the occasional celebrity yacht anchored offshore.

Another "insider" secret is Caló des Mort . Tucked away on the southern tip of the island, this tiny cove is surrounded by ochre cliffs and offers some of the best snorkeling in the Balearics.

The Perfect 10-Hour Itinerary

To maximize your boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera , we recommend the following timeline:

  • 09:00 AM: Departure from Port d'Andratx. Enjoy a light breakfast on deck.
  • 11:30 AM: Arrival at Es Palmador for the first swim of the day.
  • 01:30 PM: Tender transfer to Juan y Andrea for a world-class lunch.
  • 04:00 PM: Relaxation on the yacht's sunbeds, cruising the coast of Formentera toward Cala Saona.
  • 06:00 PM: Sunset cocktails at the anchorage before the return crossing.
  • 08:30 PM: Arrival back in Mallorca as the stars appear.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to cross from Mallorca to Formentera in a small boat?

We do not recommend making this crossing in anything smaller than 35ft. The Balearic Channel is subject to sudden changes in wind. All Elite Luxury Bookings vessels are chosen for their sea-keeping capabilities and are helmed by experienced captains.

Can we stay overnight in Formentera?

Yes. Many of our clients choose to extend their day trip into a weekend. We can arrange for your yacht to stay at a buoy in Ses Illetes or secure a rare berth in the Marina Formentera.

What should we bring on our Formentera boat trip?

Sun protection is vital, as the reflection off the white sands and turquoise water is intense. We provide towels and refreshments, so you only need your swimwear and a high-end camera to capture the scenery.
