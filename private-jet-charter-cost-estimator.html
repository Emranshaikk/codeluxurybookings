Private Jet Charter Cost Estimator

Last Updated: May 2026 • Estimated Read Time: 8 min

Featured Snippet: How Much is a Private Jet Per Hour?

The cost to charter a private jet depends heavily on the aircraft class. In 2026, expect to pay approximately $2,500 - $3,500 per hour for a Light Jet (e.g., Phenom 300), $4,500 - $6,000 per hour for a Midsize Jet (e.g., Citation Latitude), and $8,000 - $14,000+ per hour for a Heavy/Ultra-Long-Range Jet (e.g., Gulfstream G650). Prices exclude repositioning fees, landing taxes, and overnight crew costs.

Unlike booking a first-class ticket on a commercial airline, chartering a private jet does not come with a static price tag. The final invoice for a private flight is a dynamic equation factoring in the aircraft's hourly rate, the distance flown, the time it spends idling on the tarmac, and the fees charged by the specific airports you utilize.

For high-net-worth individuals and corporate executives, understanding these cost metrics is vital to optimizing aviation budgets and selecting the correct aircraft for the mission.

This ultimate 2026 Private Jet Charter Cost Guide breaks down exactly what you are paying for, category by category, so you can estimate the exact cost of your next flight.

1. Average Hourly Rates by Jet Class

The foundation of any private jet quote is the base hourly rate of the aircraft. This rate primarily covers the mechanical depreciation, maintenance, and basic operation of the specific plane.

  • Turboprops (e.g., Pilatus PC-12): $1,500 - $2,500 per hour. Perfect for short regional hops (1-2 hours) into airports with very short runways. They seat 6-8 passengers.
  • Light Jets (e.g., Phenom 300, Citation CJ4): $2,500 - $3,500 per hour. The workhorses of private aviation. Ideal for 2-3 hour flights with 6-7 passengers. Limited luggage capacity and usually no flight attendant.
  • Midsize & Super-Midsize Jets (e.g., Citation Latitude, Challenger 350): $4,500 - $7,000 per hour. These offer stand-up cabins, greater luggage capacity, coast-to-coast range, and a dedicated flight attendant. Seats up to 9 passengers.
  • Heavy & Ultra-Long-Range Jets (e.g., Gulfstream G650, Global 7500): $8,000 - $14,000+ per hour. The pinnacle of luxury aviation. These jets feature multiple cabin zones, full galleys, beds, and intercontinental range (e.g., London to Los Angeles direct). Seats 12-16 passengers.

2. Hidden Fees: What the Hourly Rate Doesn't Cover

A common misconception is multiplying the flight time by the hourly rate and assuming that is the final price. A professional charter quote will also include the following variables:

Repositioning Fees: If the jet is currently sitting in Miami, but you need to depart from Aspen, you must pay the hourly rate for the jet to fly "empty" from Miami to Aspen to pick you up.

Landing and Handling Fees: Every airport charges a fee to land, which is usually based on the weight of the aircraft. Furthermore, the FBO (Fixed Base Operator - the private terminal) charges a handling fee to park the jet and manage your luggage. Landing a Gulfstream at London Heathrow is significantly more expensive than landing a Light Jet at a regional airfield.

Crew Overnight Fees: If you are flying somewhere for a 3-day weekend and keeping the plane waiting for you, you must pay for the pilots' and flight attendant's hotel, meals, and per-diem for those three days.

Federal Excise Tax (FET) & Segment Fees: For domestic flights within the United States, there is a mandatory 7.5% Federal Excise Tax applied to the entire charter invoice.

3. The Exception: Empty Leg Flights

If you want to fly private but are highly flexible on dates and times, Empty Leg Flights offer massive discounts—sometimes up to 75% off the standard charter rate.

An empty leg occurs when a jet is booked for a one-way flight and needs to return to its home base (or its next pickup location) empty. To mitigate the loss, operators will sell this repositioning flight at a drastically reduced cost. The catch? If the original client cancels their flight, your empty leg flight is automatically canceled as well.

4. Real-World Route Cost Examples

To put this into perspective, here are estimated costs for popular high-net-worth routes in 2026:

  • London to Paris (Light Jet): Approx. $7,000 - $9,000
  • New York to Miami (Midsize Jet): Approx. $18,000 - $24,000
  • Los Angeles to Las Vegas (Light Jet): Approx. $8,000 - $11,000
  • Dubai to London (Heavy Jet): Approx. $85,000 - $110,000

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average hourly rate for a private jet charter in 2026?

Hourly rates vary by aircraft class: Light Jets typically range from $3,500 to $5,500, Midsize Jets from $6,000 to $9,000, and Heavy Jets from $12,000 to over $20,000 per billable hour.

What are the most common hidden fees in jet chartering?

Common additional costs include landing fees, de-icing (in winter), repositioning fees (ferry time), and high-end concierge catering. Elite Luxury Bookings provides transparent quotes that include these variables.

How does the jet class affect the total charter price?

Jet class is the primary price driver. A Heavy Jet has significantly higher fuel burn, crew requirements, and maintenance overhead than a Light Jet, often resulting in a 3x price difference for the same route.
