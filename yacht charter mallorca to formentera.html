The 7 Best Sailing Routes

The classic direct passage is just one of many ways to complete a yacht charter Mallorca to Formentera. These seven routes represent the full spectrum of this extraordinary crossing.

01

The Classic Direct — Palma to Formentera

Depart Palma marina south-southwest toward Ibiza and on to Formentera. Approximately 75 nautical miles, 10–12 hours under sail. The most direct yacht rental Mallorca Formentera passage for those who want maximum time at the destination.

02

The Island Passage — Via Cabrera & Ibiza

Through the Cabrera Archipelago National Park — an extraordinary protected marine reserve — before continuing to Ibiza's south coast and the final leg to Formentera. The most popular multi-day yacht charter Mallorca to Formentera itinerary among experienced sailors.

03

South Coast Explorer — Portocolom to Formentera

Begin from Portocolom on Mallorca's east coast — one of the finest natural harbours in the Balearics. Reduces passage time and awards more days exploring Formentera's 69-kilometre coastline.

04

The Overnight Crossing — Sunset Departure

Depart at dusk and arrive at sunrise. The Balearic Channel at night offers absolute darkness, an unobstructed firmament, and the rare calm of navigating by moonlight. Widely regarded as the most romantic way to make this crossing.

05

The Full Balearic Loop

For charterers with 10–14 days: Mallorca → Menorca → Ibiza → Formentera → Mallorca. Over 300 nautical miles. Four distinct islands. The definitive Balearic sailing experience for those who refuse to settle.

06

Luxury Anchorage Route — Via Es Trenc

Follow Mallorca's southern shoreline with an overnight anchorage at wild Es Trenc beach before the crossing. Ideal for catamarans and families who prefer unhurried day sails with dramatic anchorage spots each evening.

07

Express Charter — Private Motor Yacht

A motor yacht covers Mallorca to Formentera in 3–4 hours. Ideal for weekend charters and those who require maximum time at the destination. See our luxury motor yacht rentals for available vessels.

A 7-Day Sailing Itinerary

This Mallorca Formentera sailing itinerary achieves the optimal balance of passage miles, island exploration, and genuine rest.

Day One

Palma de Mallorca → Portocolom

Depart Palma marina late morning. Sail east along Mallorca's dramatic southern coastline — 35 nautical miles, approximately 5–6 hours. Overnight at anchor in Portocolom, one of Mallorca's finest natural harbours.

Day Two

Portocolom → Cabrera National Park

A 15 nm sail south to the protected Cabrera Archipelago. Advance permit required — your charter operator arranges this. Swim, snorkel, explore the historic castle. Overnight at anchor.

Day Three

Cabrera → Ibiza Town

The principal crossing — 42 nm southwest to Ibiza, 6–8 hours. Explore Dalt Vila, Ibiza's UNESCO World Heritage walled city. Optional: sunset at Café del Mar.

Day Four

Ibiza South Coast — Day Sailing

Explore the extraordinary south coast coves: Cala d'Hort below the dramatic Es Vedrà rock formation, Cala Vadella, Cala Tarida. Anchor for a long lunch in turquoise water.

Day Five

Ibiza → Formentera — Ses Illetes

Just 6 nautical miles — the shortest leg of the passage. Drop anchor at Ses Illetes. This is the defining moment of any yacht charter Mallorca to Formentera experience.

Day Six

Formentera — Full Exploration

Formentera by paddleboard, bicycle, and snorkel. Sail east to Migjorn beach in the afternoon. Sundowners at anchor as the sun drops into the sea.

Day Seven

Return or Extension

Begin the return passage to Mallorca, or extend. Formentera's 69 kilometres of coastline are not surrendered easily. Explore the Mallorca to Ibiza extension for a further island-hopping option.

Insider Tips — What Experienced Sailors Know

01

Secure Anchorage Permits Early

Ses Illetes and Espalmador require advance anchoring permits in July–August. Slots are claimed months ahead. Check Formentera's official portal for current regulations.

02

Respect the Tramuntana

The Tramuntana builds rapidly in the Balearic Channel. Always consult Windy.com before departing. The experienced skipper crosses in the morning.

03

Carry Cash Onboard

Several anchorages in Cabrera and Formentera remain cash-only for mooring fees and water taxis. €200–€300 cash is prudent for the passage.

04

Engage a Local Skipper

Even qualified offshore sailors frequently elect a local professional skipper for this crossing. Local knowledge adds immeasurable value — preferred anchorages, restaurant tender access, seasonal weather.
