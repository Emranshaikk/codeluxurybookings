2026 Market Intelligence: Western vs. Eastern Mediterranean

Choosing where to charter is your first major decision. The Mediterranean is broadly divided into two distinct cruising grounds, each catering to a different luxury profile.

The Western Mediterranean: The Social Corridor

The **French Riviera**, **Italian Riviera**, and the **Balearic Islands** (Mallorca, Ibiza) define the Western Med. This is the heart of the "Social Season," where superyachts congregate in places like Monaco, St. Tropez, and Portofino. It is the destination for world-class dining, high-end shopping, and vibrant nightlife.

The Eastern Mediterranean & Adriatic

The **Greek Islands**, **Turkish Coast**, and the **Adriatic** (Croatia, Montenegro) offer a more rugged, historical, and often more private experience. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the minimum duration for a charter?

During the peak season (July-August), most luxury yachts have a 7-day minimum requirement. Shoulder months may allow for shorter durations.

What is APA in yacht chartering?

APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance) is a fund used to cover operating expenses during your charter, such as fuel, food, drinks, and port fees. It is typically 30-40% of the base charter fee.

Do I need a license to charter a boat?

No. All of our Mediterranean charters are fully crewed. Your professional captain and crew handle all navigation and safety.
