What's Included vs. Extra Costs

Many clients searching for Melbourne to Bali private jet cost receive a base charter quote and are surprised by additional expenses at invoicing. Here is a transparent breakdown of what is typically included and what you should budget for separately.

✓ Typically Included

  • Aircraft hire and flight crew (captain + first officer)
  • VVIP terminal access at Essendon Fields (YMEN)
  • Private customs and immigration at Ngurah Rai VVIP (WADD)
  • Standard in-flight catering (water, snacks, light refreshments)
  • Ground handling and parking at both terminals
  • All standard navigation and overflight permits

+ Typically Extra

  • Fuel: A$8,000–A$18,000 return (variable by oil price)
  • Premium catering: A$500–A$3,000 (chef-prepared menus)
  • Crew overnight: A$800–A$1,500/night if multi-day stay
  • Repositioning fee: if aircraft must travel to pick you up
  • Baggage oversize: surfboards, golf clubs, additional freight
  • Indonesian landing permits (APIS): A$200–A$500

Departure & Arrival Terminals

Your choice of terminal defines the beginning and end of your Melbourne to Bali private jet experience. Both cities offer dedicated VVIP infrastructure that makes the conventional airport experience entirely irrelevant.

Melbourne Departure Options

  • Essendon Fields (YMEN) — Recommended: Melbourne's primary private aviation hub, 20 minutes from the CBD. Dedicated VVIP terminal with private security, lounge, and direct apron access. No public terminal contact.
  • Avalon Airport (YMAV): Alternative option south of Melbourne. Less congested, faster ground handling for ultra-large jets. Ideal if departing from the Surf Coast or Mornington Peninsula.
  • Melbourne Tullamarine (YMML): The major international airport. Private terminal available but less exclusive. Used when specific heavy jets require the longer runway.

Bali Arrival Options

  • Ngurah Rai VVIP Terminal (WADD) — Recommended: Bali's dedicated private aviation facility offers private customs, personal meet-and-greet, and direct limousine transfer to your villa. Zero public terminal exposure.
  • General Aviation Ramp: Available for smaller aircraft. Standard customs clearance. Less privacy but available during VVIP terminal capacity constraints in peak season.

Empty Leg Opportunities: Melbourne to Bali

Melbourne to Bali is one of the Southern Hemisphere's most active private charter corridors, generating a high volume of empty leg repositioning flights that can reduce your Melbourne to Bali private jet cost by 40–75%. An empty leg occurs when an aircraft needs to reposition after dropping off a client — the operator sells these seats at a deep discount rather than fly empty.

  • Peak availability: July–October when inbound charters create the most repositioning movements
  • Typical saving: A$16,000–A$45,000 off standard charter rates
  • Flexibility required: Empty legs are typically confirmed 24–72 hours in advance
  • Best aircraft available: Midsize and Super-Midsize jets are most commonly available on this route
