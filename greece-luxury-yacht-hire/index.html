Greece Luxury Yacht Hire: What Does It Actually Cost?

Full Cost Breakdown: What You Are Paying For

  • Base Charter Fee: Covers the vessel and professional crew for the duration of your trip.
  • Advance Provisioning Allowance (APA): Usually 30-35% of the base fee. This is used to cover fuel, food, beverages, and marina fees. Any unspent balance is returned to you.
  • VAT: Currently approximately 12% for most Greek charter contracts.
  • Fuel & Marinas: Motor yachts consume significant fuel; popular hubs like Mykonos or Hydra charge premium berthing fees in peak season.

Best Greece Yacht Charter Deals: When and How to Find Them

Experience identical weather for 20-40% less by booking in the Shoulder Season (May, June, or September). Anchorages are quieter, and the overall service is more personalized. If you must travel in July or August, aim to book your vessel 6-12 months in advance to secure the highest-calibre fleet.

Pro Tips From Our Charter Experts

  • Request Itemized Quotes: Never accept a flat fee. Insist on seeing the breakdown of APA, fuel estimates, and VAT.
  • Early Departures: Arrive at popular anchorages before 2 PM to claim the prime spots in Santorini or Hydra.
  • Pack Light: Soft-soled deck shoes are a must; hard soles can damage expensive teak decks.
  • Trust Your Skipper: Their local knowledge of hidden tavernas and untracked coves is more valuable than any app.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Underestimating Total Cost: Always factor in an additional 40-50% on top of the base fee for a realistic budget.
  • Overpacking the Itinerary: Trying to visit too many islands in a week leaves no time for true relaxation. Savour each destination.
  • Skipping Insurance: A charter damage waiver protects your security depositâ€”essential for peace of mind.

  • 3 Months Out: Complete your preference sheets (food, drink, water toys).
