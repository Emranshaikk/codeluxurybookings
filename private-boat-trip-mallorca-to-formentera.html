Private Boat Trip Mallorca to Formentera: The Ultimate 2026 Guide

While the ferry between the Balearic islands is functional, the only way to truly experience the crystalline transition from the rugged cliffs of Mallorca to the turquoise shallows of Formentera is via a private boat trip. In this comprehensive 2026 guide, we explore why this crossing is the crown jewel of Mediterranean yachting.

1. Why Choose a Private Trip Over the Ferry?

The ferry from Mallorca to Formentera is often crowded, restricted by fixed schedules, and lands you in the bustling port of La Savina. A private boat trip, however, is a bespoke experience that begins the moment you step onto the teak deck.

With a private charter from Elite Luxury Bookings, you bypass the crowds and gain access to Formentera’s world-famous beaches directly from the sea. There is no need for taxis or buses; your yacht becomes your private beach club, anchored in the most exclusive waters of Europe.

2. The Crossing: Mallorca to Formentera

The journey from Mallorca (typically departing from Palma or Port d'Andratx) to Formentera is approximately 70 nautical miles. Depending on your choice of vessel, this crossing is an event in itself.

The Morning Departure

We recommend a 9:00 AM departure to catch the calmest seas. As you leave the Bay of Palma, the skyline of the La Seu Cathedral fades, replaced by the open blue of the Balearic Sea. This is the time to enjoy a chef-prepared breakfast on the aft deck while the crew prepares the water toys.

3. Selecting Your Vessel: Speed vs. Luxury

4. Secret Stops: Es Palmador & Ses Illetes

One of the primary advantages of a private boat trip is the ability to anchor at Es Palmador. This private island, located just north of Formentera, is inaccessible by ferry and remains one of the last untouched paradises in the Mediterranean.

After a morning swim in the mud baths or the crystal-clear waters of Es Palmador, your captain will navigate the short distance to Playa de Ses Illetes. Often voted the best beach in Europe, its narrow strip of white sand and shallow turquoise water is the perfect backdrop for a sunset cocktail.

Pro Tip: Lunch Reservations

Formentera’s beach restaurants, like Beso Beach and Juan y Andrea, are world-renowned. Our concierge team manages these reservations months in advance, ensuring your tender is ready to whisk you from your yacht directly to your table.

5. Pricing & Charter Logistics

Chartering a private boat for the Mallorca-Formentera route involves several cost components beyond the base fee.

  • Base Charter Fee: Covers the hire of the boat and the crew.
  • Fuel Surcharge: For the 140-mile round trip, fuel is a significant factor. Expect to pay between €500 and €3,000 depending on the engines.
  • APA (Advanced Provisioning Allowance): Usually 30% of the charter fee, used to cover food, drinks, and port fees.

6. Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a boat trip from Mallorca to Formentera?

By private speedboat or yacht, the crossing typically takes between 2 to 3.5 hours. A high-speed VanDutch or Pardo can make the trip in just under 2 hours in calm conditions.

Can you do a day trip to Formentera from Mallorca?

Absolutely. It is one of our most requested itineraries. A 10-hour charter gives you enough time for the crossing, a 3-hour lunch on the island, and several swim stops at Dragonera or Es Palmador.

What is the best time of year for this trip?

The window from late May to early October is ideal. For those seeking privacy, the "shoulder months" of June and September offer the best balance of warm water and fewer crowds at the anchorages.
