The "Plus-Plus" Model Explained

In luxury yachting, "plus-plus" refers to VAT (Value Added Tax) and the APA (Advance Provisioning Allowance). While your base fee covers the hire of the yacht and the crew's wages, it rarely covers the daily operations of your journey.

1. Advance Provisioning Allowance (APA)

The APA is typically 30-35% of the base charter fee. It acts as a bank account for your crew to handle your expenses throughout the week, including:

  • Fuel: For main engines, generators, and tenders.
  • Provisions: High-end food, spirits, and fine wines customized to your preference sheet.
  • Marina Fees: Docking in premium ports like St. Tropez or Porto Cervo.
  • Communications: Satellite internet and shore transfers.

2. VAT (Value Added Tax)

VAT varies wildly by country and sometimes by the route you take. For example, a charter starting in Italy might face a 22% VAT, whereas a Greek charter may have reduced rates depending on the navigation zone. Always ensure your quote explicitly states if VAT is included.

3. Fuel: The Silent Variable

If you choose to island-hop at 25 knots every day, your fuel bill will be exponentially higher than a leisurely cruise at 10 knots. Motor yachts can consume between 50 and 500 liters of diesel per hour. Pro Tip: Ask your captain for a fuel-efficient itinerary to maximize your APA.

4. Crew Gratuity: The Unwritten Rule

While not a mandatory "cost," tipping is standard in the yachting industry for exceptional service. The industry standard is 10% to 15% of the base charter fee, usually presented to the captain at the end of the trip for equal distribution among the crew.

FAQs: Yacht Charter Financing

What happens to unused APA funds?

At the end of your charter, the captain will provide a full breakdown of receipts. Any unused balance in the APA account is returned to you in full, usually via bank transfer or cash.

Do I pay for the crew's meals?

Yes, your APA covers the provisions for the crew. However, they typically eat simpler meals than the guests, prepared by the same chef.

Are water sports equipment extra?

Standard "toys" (paddleboards, snorkeling gear) are usually included. However, some heavy equipment like SeaBobs or E-Foils may carry a small rental fee if not part of the yacht's permanent inventory.
