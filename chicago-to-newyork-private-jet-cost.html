America's Busiest Private Jet Corridor

Chicago to New York is the single most active private jet route in the United States. Executives, finance professionals, entertainers, and sports teams fly this 709-mile corridor daily — making it the most competitive and best-served route for private aviation. Departing from Midway (KMDW) or Chicago Executive (KPWK), all flights arrive at Teterboro Airport (KTEB), the New York area's dedicated private aviation hub just 8 miles from Midtown Manhattan. According to the National Business Aviation Association (NBAA), the Chicago–New York corridor consistently ranks as the #1 busiest private jet route in North America.

What's Included vs. Extra Costs

When comparing Chicago to New York private jet costs across operators, understanding the full price structure prevents surprises. Here is a transparent breakdown of what is and is not typically included.

  • Standard in-flight catering (water, snacks, soft drinks)
  • Ground handling at both terminals

Chicago Departure & New York Arrival Terminals

Your choice of terminal is as important as your aircraft on the Chicago to New York private jet route. Here is the complete guide to every relevant private aviation facility at both ends.

Chicago Departure Options

  • Chicago Midway (KMDW) — Most Popular: Located 10 miles southwest of the Loop, Midway's private FBO terminals offer fast ramp access and are preferred by South Side, downtown, and western suburbs clients. Signature Flight Support and Million Air both operate here.
  • Chicago Executive Airport (KPWK) — North Shore Preferred: Located in Wheeling, 20 miles north of downtown. Significantly less congestion than Midway, with excellent FBO infrastructure. Ideal for clients in Lake Forest, Wilmette, Evanston, or the northern suburbs.
  • DuPage Airport (KDPA) — Western Suburbs: Located in West Chicago, serving Naperville, Oak Brook, and western corridor clients. A quieter alternative with good operator availability.

New York Arrival Options

  • Teterboro Airport (KTEB) — Primary Choice: America's busiest private jet airport, located just 8 miles from Midtown Manhattan in New Jersey. Dedicated FBO terminals (Signature, Jet Aviation), zero commercial traffic, 20-minute transfer to Midtown, 25 minutes to the Upper East Side.
  • Westchester County Airport (KHPN) — Greenwich & Upper East Side: Located 30 miles north of Midtown. Preferred by clients destined for Greenwich, the Hamptons, or the Upper East Side. Quieter than Teterboro during peak periods.
  • Republic Airport (KFRG) — Long Island & Hamptons: Best option for clients heading to the Hamptons, Long Island, or JFK-area destinations. Significantly less congested than Teterboro.

Frequently Asked Questions

Chicago to New York private jet cost, logistics, and booking questions answered

How much does a Chicago to New York private jet cost?

The Chicago to New York private jet cost starts from $7,000–$12,000 one-way for a Very Light Jet, $10,000–$18,000 for a Light Jet, $16,000–$26,000 for a Midsize Jet, and $24,000–$36,000 for a Super-Midsize. Peak business season (September–November) pricing is typically 20–30% higher. Fuel is generally included on this short 709-mile route. For a precise quote, use our 2026 Cost Estimator.

How long is the flight from Chicago to New York by private jet?

The Chicago to New York private jet flight takes approximately 1 hour 58 minutes non-stop, covering 709 miles. No fuel stop is required on any aircraft class. Total door-to-door time from a Chicago FBO to Midtown Manhattan is typically 3–3.5 hours — compared to 5–6+ hours via commercial aviation when you include security, boarding, and baggage.

Which airport do private jets use for New York from Chicago?

Teterboro Airport (KTEB) is the primary arrival point for private jets from Chicago — 8 miles from Midtown with no commercial traffic and 20-minute transfers to most Manhattan destinations. For clients heading north or east of the city, Westchester County Airport (KHPN) saves significant ground transfer time. For the Hamptons or Long Island, Republic Airport (KFRG) is optimal.

Which Chicago airport is best for a private jet to New York?

Chicago Midway (KMDW) is most commonly used, located 10 miles from the Loop with multiple FBO options. Chicago Executive Airport (KPWK) is strongly preferred by North Shore and northern suburbs clients — it's less congested and has excellent facilities. DuPage Airport (KDPA) serves western suburbs clients.
