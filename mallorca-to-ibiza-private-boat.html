⚓ Quick Answer: Mallorca to Ibiza Private Boat

A private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza costs from €800/day for a speedboat to €25,000+/day for a crewed superyacht. The 150 km Balearic crossing takes 3–5 hours depending on vessel. The optimal departure point is Port d'Andratx — the closest major marina to Ibiza, saving up to 45 minutes versus Palma.

Planning a Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing is one of the finest decisions you can make for a Mediterranean summer. While thousands of travellers crowd the ferry terminals in Palma each July, the elite traveller steps aboard a private vessel and glides across the Balearic Sea on their own terms — no queues, no strangers, no compromises.

A private boat charter from Mallorca to Ibiza is far more than a transfer between two islands. It is the journey itself: the rush of open water, the freedom to drop anchor at an uninhabited cove, and the privilege of arriving at Ibiza's most exclusive marinas exactly when you choose. In this complete 2026 guide, Elite Luxury Bookings covers costs, vessel types, departure marinas, and everything you need to book the perfect Mallorca to Ibiza private boat.

1. Distance & Journey Time: The 150 km Balearic Gap

The distance from Mallorca to Ibiza is approximately 150 km (93 nautical miles) across open Mediterranean water. The Balearic Sea can present varying sea states between May and September — glassy calms in early morning, occasional afternoon chop by midsummer. Choosing the right vessel for your Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing is the single most important factor determining both journey time and on-board comfort.

Vessel Type Journey Time Cruising Speed Best For
Speedboat / RIB 3 – 3.5 hrs 30–40 knots Couples, fast transfers
Motor Yacht (40–60ft) 4 – 5 hrs 18–25 knots Groups of 6–12
Luxury Catamaran 5 – 8 hrs 10–14 knots Families, stability
Sailing Yacht 7 – 10 hrs 6–10 knots Purists, overnight trips
Superyacht (60ft+) 4 – 5 hrs 18–28 knots VIP groups, multi-day

Pro tip: Depart before 9am from Port d'Andratx to take advantage of calm morning seas. Afternoon crossings (after 2pm) can experience up to Force 3–4 Beaufort winds, adding 30–45 minutes to your crossing time.

2. Private Boat Mallorca to Ibiza: Cost Guide

Budgeting for a private boat Mallorca to Ibiza depends on the season, vessel category, and whether you require a fully crewed charter or a bareboat hire. Peak season (July and August) prices are typically 30–50% higher than shoulder months. According to the Boat International charter cost guide, Mediterranean inter-island charter rates have risen approximately 18% since 2023.

Cost by Vessel Type — Rates

  • Speedboat / RIB: €800 – €2,500 per day. Ideal for fast point-to-point transfers for couples or small groups of up to 8. Fuel typically adds €600–€1,200 return.
  • Motor Yacht (30–45ft): €1,500 – €5,000 per day. The benchmark Mallorca to Ibiza private boat choice for comfortable crossings with groups of 8–10. Most include a skipper.
  • Luxury Catamaran: €2,000 – €7,000 per day. Outstanding deck space and superior stability. Perfect for families or guests prone to sea sickness.
  • Superyacht (60ft+): €8,000 – €25,000+ per day. The complete VIP experience with full crew, master cabins, on-board chef, and tender.
  • Gulet / Traditional: €1,800 – €4,500 per day. A slower, atmospheric option for those who want to savour the Balearic Sea rather than sprint across it.

Fuel costs are usually separate. For a return Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing, budget €1,200–€4,500 for fuel depending on vessel size and engine type. Always confirm the fuel policy before signing your charter agreement. For a full breakdown of all additional costs, see our 2026 Superyacht Cost Masterclass.

3. The 5 Best Vessel Types for a Mallorca to Ibiza Private Boat

Not every boat is suited to a 93-mile open-water crossing. At Elite Luxury Bookings, every vessel we recommend for a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza has been assessed for speed, stability, passenger comfort, and arrival presence. Here are the five that excel on this specific route.

1. The Chase Boat (Wajer 55 / VanDutch 55)

These are the status symbols of the Mediterranean. At 30+ knots, a Wajer 55 cuts the Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing to just over 3 hours while remaining remarkably stable in moderate chop. The open-air deck, carbon-fibre hull, and minimalist Scandinavian interior make this the most photographed vessel in the Balearics. Expect €2,500–€5,000/day including skipper.

2. The Flybridge Motor Yacht (50–65ft)

For groups of 8–14 who want multiple sundecks, a shaded salon, and the option to overnight in Ibiza before returning to Mallorca, the flybridge motor yacht is the definitive private boat Mallorca to Ibiza choice. The elevated flybridge provides 360° views across the Balearic Sea, while the main deck salon offers an air-conditioned retreat. Rates: €4,500–€12,000/day.

3. The Luxury Catamaran

Families chartering a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza with children or guests sensitive to motion consistently choose catamarans. The wide beam eliminates rolling, and the generous deck space provides the freedom to move around during the crossing. An added bonus: catamarans can anchor in shallow-water coves near Ibiza that monohulls cannot reach. Rates: €3,000–€8,000/day.

4. The Gulet (Traditional Turkish Wooden Yacht)

A gulet is the romantic alternative — handcrafted wooden decks, a shaded rear awning, and a pace that invites you to truly experience the Mediterranean rather than transit it. While the crossing takes longer at 6–8 hours, the atmosphere aboard is incomparable. This is the preferred vessel for honeymoon or anniversary Mallorca to Ibiza private boat charters. Rates: €1,800–€4,500/day.

5. The Superyacht (60ft+)

For those who regard the Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing as just one chapter in a multi-day Balearic itinerary, a crewed superyacht delivers unmatched luxury. A dedicated captain, chef, and crew of 3–6 attend to every detail. Wake up anchored off Formentera, cross to Ibiza for dinner at Nobu, return to Mallorca for a private beach club arrival. Rates: €8,000–€25,000+/day, plus APA.

4. Best Departure & Arrival Points for a Private Boat Mallorca to Ibiza

Your choice of marina defines the character of your private boat trip from Mallorca to Ibiza. According to Yacht Harbour's Balearic marina guide, the southwestern marinas of Mallorca consistently rank highest for inter-island crossings due to proximity, facilities, and luxury vessel availability.

Mallorca Departure Points

  • Port d'Andratx: The premier departure point for any Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing. Located on the southwestern tip of Mallorca, it is the closest major marina to Ibiza — saving approximately 45 minutes of open-water time versus departing from Palma.
  • Puerto Portals: Known as "Millionaire's Row," this is Mallorca's most glamorous marina and offers the widest selection of luxury yachts for hire. The 30-minute drive from Palma Airport makes it the top choice for fly-and-sail clients.
  • Port Adriano: Designed by Philippe Starck, this ultra-modern marina is the most architecturally striking departure point on the island. Its deep-water berths accommodate superyachts to 80m.

Ibiza Arrival Points

  • Marina Botafoch: Positioned directly opposite Ibiza Town's historic Dalt Vila, this is the most coveted arrival berth on the island. Restaurants, nightlife, and the old city are all within walking distance.
  • Cala Jondal (Tender Arrival): The VIP choice for those headed to Blue Marlin Ibiza. We arrange a dedicated tender to collect you from the anchorage — you step ashore without touching a marina berth.
  • Santa Eulalia Marina: A quieter, more refined arrival on Ibiza's eastern coast. Perfect for villa-based travellers who prefer a peaceful atmosphere over the intensity of Ibiza Town.
  • Formentera Day Stop: A private boat Mallorca to Ibiza crossing allows you to stop at Formentera's crystal shallows — a privilege no ferry offers. Anchor at Ses Illetes for 2 hours before completing the final leg to Ibiza.

5. Best Time to Charter a Private Boat from Mallorca to Ibiza

Timing your Mallorca to Ibiza private boat charter correctly can save you thousands of euros and deliver a far superior experience. The Balearic season runs from April to October, with each month offering distinct advantages.

May & June — The Connoisseur's Choice

Sea temperatures of 20–22°C, virtually no crowds, and charter rates 25–35% below peak levels. Ibiza's beach clubs are open but not yet overwhelmed. June in particular delivers the best weather reliability for a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza crossing — calm seas and long evenings of golden Mediterranean light.

July & August — Peak Season

The most vibrant period to make the Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing. Both islands are at full energy — the beach clubs, restaurants, and marinas are buzzing. However, availability is extremely limited and prices are 30–50% higher. Book at least 3–4 months in advance for July and August charters. The Balearic Islands Tourism Authority confirms that peak season visitor numbers hit record highs in 2025, and 2026 is expected to match or exceed those figures.

September — The Insider's Window

September is widely considered the finest month for an elite private boat Mallorca to Ibiza charter. Prices begin to ease, crowds thin, but the sea is at its warmest (24–25°C) and the weather remains highly reliable. Many experienced travellers time their Balearic crossing specifically for the first two weeks of September for this reason.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza cost currently?

A private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza ranges from €800/day for a speedboat or RIB to €25,000+/day for a fully crewed superyacht. A motor yacht — the most popular choice — costs €1,500–€5,000/day including skipper. Peak summer (July–August) rates are 30–50% higher than shoulder season. Fuel is typically charged separately and ranges from €1,200 to €4,500 return depending on vessel size. See our full superyacht cost guide for a detailed breakdown.

How long is the private boat journey from Mallorca to Ibiza?

The Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing covers 150 km (93 nautical miles). A speedboat completes this in 3–3.5 hours at 30–40 knots. A motor yacht takes 4–5 hours at 18–25 knots. A luxury catamaran takes 5–8 hours. A sailing yacht may take 7–10 hours. We recommend departing before 9am to benefit from calmer morning sea conditions.

What is the best marina to depart from in Mallorca for Ibiza?

Port d'Andratx on the southwestern tip of Mallorca is the optimal departure point for any private boat to Ibiza. It is the closest major marina to Ibiza, saving approximately 45 minutes versus departing from Palma de Mallorca. Puerto Portals and Port Adriano are excellent premium alternatives offering outstanding luxury vessel selection.

Is a private boat better than the ferry from Mallorca to Ibiza?

For groups of 6 or more, a private boat Mallorca to Ibiza is almost always the superior choice. The cost per person is often comparable to Gold Class ferry tickets, but the experience is incomparable: total privacy, flexible departure, stops at Espalmador and Formentera, and a genuine sense of occasion. Explore our full luxury yacht fleet to find the right vessel.

When is the best time to charter a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza?

May, June, and September offer the best combination of reliable weather, calm seas, and competitive rates for a Mallorca to Ibiza private boat charter. July and August are peak season — vibrant and guaranteed sunshine, but 30–50% more expensive and requiring advance booking of 3–4 months. Early September is considered the connoisseur's choice: warmest sea temperatures, easing prices, and noticeably thinner crowds.

Can we stop at Formentera or Espalmador on a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza?

Absolutely — and this is one of the greatest advantages of booking a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza over the ferry. You have complete freedom to drop anchor at Espalmador's translucent shallows, spend 2–3 hours at Formentera's Ses Illetes beach, then continue to your Ibiza arrival marina. This multi-stop island experience is not possible on any scheduled ferry service.
