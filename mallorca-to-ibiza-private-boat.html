Dreaming of gliding across the sparkling Mediterranean from Mallorca to Ibiza on your own private boat? You're not alone. 1. Distance & Journey Time: Mastering the 150 km Gap

The distance from Mallorca to Ibiza is approximately 150 km (93 miles) across open Mediterranean water. While the Balearics are geographically close, the sea state can vary significantly. Your choice of vessel directly impacts your journey time and comfort level.

Vessel Type Approx. Journey Time Cruising Speed
Speedboat / RIB 3 – 3.5 Hours 30–40 Knots
Motor Yacht (40ft-60ft) 4 – 5 Hours 18–25 Knots
Catamaran 5 – 8 Hours 10–14 Knots
Sailing Yacht 7 – 10 Hours 6–10 Knots

For those looking for the "Elite" experience, we recommend departing from Port d'Andratx . Located on the southwestern tip of Mallorca, it is the closest major port to Ibiza, saving you nearly 45 minutes of travel time compared to departing from Palma de Mallorca.

2. Mallorca to Ibiza Private Boat Cost: 2026 Price Report

Budgeting for a private boat Mallorca to Ibiza trip depends on the season and the level of luxury required. Prices in July and August can be 30-50% higher than the shoulder season (May or September).

The Baseline: Cost by Vessel Type

  • Speedboat / RIB: €800 – €2,500 per day. Best for fast point-to-point transfers for couples or small groups.
  • Motor Yacht (30–45ft): €1,500 – €5,000 per day. The standard for comfortable crossings with groups of 8-10.
  • Luxury Catamaran: €2,000 – €7,000 per day. Perfect for families seeking stability and massive deck space.
  • Superyacht (60ft+): €8,000 – €25,000+ per day. The VIP choice featuring full crew, master cabins, and onboard chefs.

Note on Fuel: Most charters are quoted "ex-fuel." For a return crossing from Mallorca to Ibiza, expect fuel costs to range from €1,200 for a single-engine boat to over €4,000 for a large motor yacht.

3. Best Boat Types for the Route

Not all boats are designed for a 90-mile open-water crossing. At Elite Luxury Bookings, we recommend two primary styles for this route:

The Chase Boat (Wajer 55 or VanDutch 55)

These are the "status symbols" of the Mediterranean. They are fast (30+ knots), extremely stable, and designed for day trips. A private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza on a Wajer 55 is the smoothest ride in the Balearics. It slices through the afternoon chop, ensuring you arrive in Ibiza Town or Cala Jondal in peak form.

The Flybridge Motor Yacht

If you prefer a more traditional luxury experience, a flybridge yacht offers multiple levels for sunbathing and a more sheltered interior. These are better suited if you plan to stay overnight in Ibiza before returning to Mallorca.

4. Strategic Departure & Arrival Points

Your choice of marina dictates the start and end of your story. In 2026, we have identified the premier hubs for elite travelers.

Mallorca Departure Points:

  • Port d'Andratx: Closest to Ibiza. Best for speed.
  • Puerto Portals: The "Millionaire's Row" of Mallorca. Best for luxury yacht selection.
  • Port Adriano: Philippe Starck-designed marina, offering ultra-modern facilities for superyachts.

Ibiza Arrival Points:

  • Marina Botafoch: Located in Ibiza Town, overlooking Dalt Vila. The best hub for nightlife and restaurants.
  • Cala Jondal: The VIP choice. We can arrange for a tender to meet you at anchor and take you directly to Blue Marlin Ibiza or Jondal.
  • Santa Eulalia: A quieter, more refined arrival point on the eastern coast of Ibiza.

5. Private Boat vs. Ferry: The Elite Comparison

While the Balearia and Trasmediterranea ferries serve thousands daily, they cannot match the private boat Mallorca to Ibiza experience. For a group of 8 people, the cost of a private charter often approaches the price of 8 "Gold Class" ferry tickets, but with 100% more privacy and flexibility.

A private boat allows you to stop at Espalmador —the uninhabited island between Ibiza and Formentera—for a swim in crystal clear water that no ferry can access. It turns a boring transit into the best day of your holiday.
