1. The Strategic Point of Departure: Why Port d'Andratx?

For the uninitiated, Palma is the default starting point. For the elite, the journey begins in Port d'Andratx. Located on the southwestern tip of Mallorca, Andratx is the closest major port to Ibiza. Starting here saves approximately 45 minutes of open-water cruising, which is the difference between arriving for the first champagne of lunch and being late for your reservation.

Our ground team coordinates luxury transfers from any Mallorca villa or the Son Bonet private jet terminal directly to the dock, ensuring your transition from land to sea is instantaneous.

2. The Icons of the Crossing: Wajer vs. VanDutch

Choosing the right vessel for a 70-mile open-water crossing is a technical decision as much as an aesthetic one. In 2026, two models dominate the Balearic horizon.

The Wajer 55

Commonly referred to as the "Rolls Royce of the Sea," the Wajer 55 is designed for the long-haul "Day Trip." Its hull is engineered to slice through the typical afternoon Mediterranean chop, ensuring your guests arrive in Ibiza without a hair out of place.

The VanDutch 55

The status symbol of the Mediterranean. With its aggressive plumb bow and massive open deck, it is the ultimate "Social Boat." It is the preferred choice for those traveling from Mallorca to Ibiza for a high-energy day at a beach club.

3. VIP Arrivals: The Cala Jondal Protocol

Most travelers arrive in Ibiza Town, but the true elite arrive in Cala Jondal. This south-facing bay is the home of Blue Marlin Ibiza and Jondal. Entering this bay on a 55ft chase boat is the only way to arrive.

At Elite Luxury Bookings, we synchronize your arrival with the venue's tender service. As you approach the bay, our captain notifies the club, and a professional tender meets you at your anchor point to whisk you directly to the beach club pier.

4. Technical Logistics: Fuel, Sea States & Timing

The crossing requires a vessel with "Long Legs." At a cruising speed of 28 knots, you are looking at a 2.5-hour journey. We strictly monitor the "Tramuntana" winds, which can create a following sea on the return journey. Our captains are instructed to depart Mallorca by 10:00 AM to ensure the calmest possible crossing.

5. The 'Golden Route' Itinerary

To maximize a private boat trip to Ibiza, we recommend the following 12-hour mission:

  • 09:30: Boarding at Port d'Andratx.
  • 12:00: Arrival at Espalmador (The uninhabited island between Ibiza and Formentera) for a pre-lunch swim in turquoise water.
  • 13:30: Arrival at Cala Jondal for a long, late lunch.
  • 17:30: Sunset cruise past Es Vedrà—the most magnetic rock in the world.
  • 18:30: High-speed return crossing to Mallorca.

6. Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to cross from Mallorca to Ibiza in a small boat?

Safety is entirely dependent on the vessel's hull design and the captain's expertise. We only use offshore-certified chase boats (Category B or A) that are specifically engineered for open-sea crossings. We never attempt the crossing if significant wave heights exceed 1.5 meters.

Can I book a one-way trip to Ibiza?

Yes. This is a very common request for clients moving their base from a Mallorca villa to an Ibiza estate. We handle all luggage transfers on board and can coordinate a VIP chauffeur to meet you at any Ibiza marina.

What should I wear for the crossing?

While it is hot on the islands, the wind at 30 knots can be cool. We recommend a light luxury windbreaker or pashmina for the crossing itself, along with your high-end swimwear for arrival.
