Quick Answer: Mallorca to Ibiza Private Boat

A private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza costs from €800/day for a speedboat to €25,000+/day for a luxury superyacht. The 150 km crossing takes 3–5 hours. The best departure point is Port d'Andratx — the closest major marina to Ibiza.

Dreaming of gliding across the sparkling Mediterranean on a Mallorca to Ibiza private boat? You are not alone. Every summer, thousands of travellers make this iconic Balearic crossing — and the elite skip the crowded ferries entirely. A private boat charter from Mallorca to Ibiza is not simply a transfer; it is the ultimate expression of Mediterranean freedom.

In this authority guide, Elite Luxury Bookings breaks down exactly how much a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza costs, which vessels perform best on the 150 km open-water crossing, and how to secure the most stylish arrival on the White Isle. 1. Distance & Journey Time: Mastering the 150 km Gap

The distance from Mallorca to Ibiza is approximately 150 km (93 miles) across open Mediterranean water. While the Balearic Islands sit in relative proximity, sea state can vary significantly between May and September. Your choice of vessel for a Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing directly determines both your journey time and on-board comfort.

Vessel Type Approx. Journey Time Cruising Speed
Speedboat / RIB 3 – 3.5 Hours 30–40 Knots
Motor Yacht (40ft–60ft) 4 – 5 Hours 18–25 Knots
Catamaran 5 – 8 Hours 10–14 Knots
Sailing Yacht 7 – 10 Hours 6–10 Knots

For the optimal experience, we recommend departing from Port d'Andratx on the southwestern tip of Mallorca — the closest major port to Ibiza, saving nearly 45 minutes versus departing from Palma de Mallorca. According to Yacht Harbour's Balearic marina guide, Port d'Andratx consistently ranks among the top departure marinas for inter-island crossings.

2. Mallorca to Ibiza Private Boat Cost: 2026 Price Report

Budgeting for a private boat Mallorca to Ibiza depends on the season, vessel category, and whether you require a crewed charter. Prices in July and August are typically 30–50% higher than shoulder season (May or September). The Boat International charter cost guide provides an independent view of how Mediterranean rates compare globally.

The Baseline: Cost by Vessel Type

  • Speedboat / RIB: €800 – €2,500 per day. Best for fast point-to-point transfers for couples or small groups.
  • Motor Yacht (30–45ft): €1,500 – €5,000 per day. The standard for comfortable crossings with groups of 8–10.
  • Luxury Catamaran: €2,000 – €7,000 per day. Perfect for families seeking stability and expansive deck space.
  • Superyacht (60ft+): €8,000 – €25,000+ per day. The VIP choice with full crew, master cabins, and an onboard chef.

Note on Fuel: Most charters are quoted "ex-fuel." For a return Mallorca to Ibiza private boat crossing, expect fuel costs ranging from €1,200 for a single-engine vessel to over €4,000 for a large motor yacht. Always confirm the fuel policy when requesting your quote. For a deeper breakdown of all costs involved, see our 2026 Superyacht Cost Masterclass.

3. Best Boat Types for the Mallorca to Ibiza Route

Not every vessel is suited to a 90-mile open-water crossing. When planning your private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza, vessel selection is as important as the journey itself. At Elite Luxury Bookings, we recommend two primary styles for this specific route:

The Chase Boat (Wajer 55 or VanDutch 55)

These are the status symbols of the Mediterranean. Fast at 30+ knots, extremely stable, and designed specifically for day crossings. A Mallorca to Ibiza private boat charter aboard a Wajer 55 is the smoothest ride in the Balearics. It cuts cleanly through afternoon chop, ensuring you arrive at Ibiza Town or Cala Jondal in peak form.

The Flybridge Motor Yacht

If you prefer a traditional luxury experience, a flybridge motor yacht offers multiple sundecks and a sheltered interior salon. These vessels are better suited if you plan to overnight in Ibiza before the return crossing to Mallorca. For a full vessel comparison, see our guide to Motor vs Sailing Yacht Charter.

4. Strategic Departure & Arrival Points

Your choice of marina defines the start and end of your private boat trip from Mallorca to Ibiza. In 2026, these are the premier hubs we recommend to elite travellers.

Mallorca Departure Points

  • Port d'Andratx: Closest to Ibiza. Best for speed and minimising open-water time.
  • Puerto Portals: The "Millionaire's Row" of Mallorca. Best for luxury yacht selection and high-end facilities.
  • Port Adriano: Philippe Starck-designed marina offering ultra-modern superyacht berths.

Ibiza Arrival Points

  • Marina Botafoch: Located in Ibiza Town overlooking Dalt Vila. The prime hub for nightlife, restaurants, and the old city.
  • Cala Jondal: The VIP choice. We arrange a tender to collect you at anchor and transport you directly to Blue Marlin Ibiza.
  • Santa Eulalia: A quieter, more refined arrival on the eastern coast — ideal for villa-based travellers.

5. Private Boat vs. Ferry: The Elite Comparison

While Balearia and Trasmediterranea ferries carry thousands of passengers daily, they cannot match the private boat Mallorca to Ibiza experience. For a group of 8, the cost of a private charter often approaches the price of 8 Gold Class ferry tickets — but with complete privacy, your own schedule, and zero crowds.

A private boat trip from Mallorca to Ibiza also lets you stop at Espalmador — the pristine uninhabited island between Ibiza and Formentera — for a swim in crystal-clear shallows that no ferry can reach. What begins as a transfer becomes the highlight of your holiday. You can read more about Espalmador and the Balearics via the official Balearic Islands tourism authority.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza cost?

A private boat from Mallorca to Ibiza costs between €800 and €25,000+ per day depending on vessel category. A speedboat or RIB starts from €800/day, a motor yacht from €1,500/day, a catamaran from €2,000/day, and a crewed superyacht from €8,000/day. Peak summer prices (July–August) are 30–50% higher. Fuel is typically quoted separately and ranges from €1,200 to €4,000+ for the return crossing.

How long is the boat journey from Mallorca to Ibiza?

The Mallorca to Ibiza private boat journey takes 3–5 hours depending on vessel type. A high-performance speedboat completes the 150 km (93-mile) crossing in 3–3.5 hours at 30–40 knots. A motor yacht takes 4–5 hours at 18–25 knots. A sailing yacht may take 7–10 hours.

What is the best marina to depart from in Mallorca?

Port d'Andratx, on the southwestern tip of Mallorca, is the ideal departure point for a private boat to Ibiza. It is the closest major marina to Ibiza, saving approximately 45 minutes of crossing time versus departing from Palma. Puerto Portals and Port Adriano are premium alternatives with outstanding luxury vessel availability.

Is a private boat better than the ferry from Mallorca to Ibiza?

For groups of 6 or more, a private boat Mallorca to Ibiza offers better value than Gold Class ferry tickets when you factor in total privacy, flexible departure times, and the ability to stop at uninhabited islands like Espalmador.
