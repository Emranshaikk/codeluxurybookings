Selecting from the **different types of private jets** is the first step in optimizing your mission. Whether you are performing a short intra-European hop or a transcontinental business mission, each aircraft class offers a unique balance of **range, speed, seating capacity, and cabin luxury**. Understanding these categories ensures your ROI is maximized every flight.

The 6 Categories of Luxury Performance

Our operations team analyzes the global fleet based on mission specific requirements for our elite clients:

1. Very Light Jets (VLJs)

Example: Citation Mustang, Phenom 100.
Ideal for short trips (1-2 hours) with 4-5 passengers. VLJs provide the luxury of private flight with the accessibility of smaller regional airports.

2. Light Jets

Example: Phenom 300, Citation CJ3+.
The industry standard for "Inter-City" travel. Offering 7-8 seats and a 2,000 nautical mile range, perfect for routes like London to Nice or Miami to New York.

3. Midsize Jets

Example: Hawker 800XP,Citation Latitude.
Focusing on "Stand-up Cabins" and a full galley. Midsize jets offer the space needed for collaborative corporate missions with 8-9 passengers.

4. Super-Midsize Jets

Example: Challenger 350, Gulfstream G280.
The "Mastery" category. Exceptional range (3,000+ nautical miles) combined with quiet cabins and high-speed performance for transcontinental missions.

5. Large Cabin / Heavy Jets

Example: Global 6000, Gulfstream G450.
Infinite luxury. Featuring multiple living zones, flat-bed seating, and dedicated flight attendants for 12-16 passengers.

6. Ultra-Long Range Superjets

Example: Bombardier Global 7500, Gulfstream G650ER.
The pinnacle of aviation. Capable of flying 14+ hours non-stop (e.g., London to hongkong) with dedicated owner's suites and shower facilities.

FAQs: Types of Private Jets Guide

Which jet is best for a group of 6?

For a group of 6, we recommend a **Light Jet (like the Phenom 300)** for efficiency or a **Midsize Jet** if you require a stand-up cabin and more luggage capacity.

What determines the cost difference between classes?

Mainly **fuel burn and airport handling fees**. Larger jets have higher operational costs and higher landing fees at premium hubs like Teterboro or Farnborough.

Can all jet types fly overseas?

No. While Light and Midsize jets can perform "hops" (e.g., via Iceland), trans-Atlantic or trans-Pacific missions are reserved for **Super-Midsize, Heavy, and Ultra-Long Range** categories.
