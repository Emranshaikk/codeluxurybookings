What's Included vs. Extra Costs

Many clients comparing Cabo to Los Angeles private jet cost receive a base quote and are surprised by additional charges at invoicing. This is a transparent breakdown so you can budget with complete confidence.

✓ Typically Included

  • Aircraft hire and flight crew (captain + first officer)
  • Standard in-flight catering (water, soft drinks, snacks)
  • Fuel for the 922-mile non-stop crossing
  • Ground handling at both MMSD and KVNY
  • Basic customs and immigration clearance assistance
  • All standard navigation and overflight permits

+ Typically Extra

  • Premium catering: $300–$1,500 (chef-curated menus, champagne service)
  • Crew overnight: $600–$1,200/night if multi-day trip
  • Repositioning fee: if aircraft must travel to pick you up
  • International handling at MMSD: $200–$500 (Mexico customs facilitation)
  • Ground transport coordination: $150–$400 (limo/SUV at KVNY)
  • Pet transport: varies by animal size and operator policy

Departure & Arrival Terminals

Choosing the right terminal is what separates a seamless private jet from Cabo to Los Angeles experience from a frustrating one. Both ends of this route have dedicated private aviation infrastructure.

Cabo San Lucas Departure

  • Los Cabos International Airport (MMSD) — Only Option: All private jets depart from the general aviation ramp at MMSD. Elite operators can arrange full private handling, customs pre-clearance, and airside vehicle access to minimise exposure to the commercial terminal entirely. Our concierge arranges all Mexican customs paperwork in advance.

Los Angeles Arrival Options

  • Van Nuys Airport (KVNY) — Recommended: The gold standard for private aviation in LA. Dedicated FBO terminals (Signature Flight Support, Atlantic Aviation), no commercial traffic, faster customs clearance, and direct ramp-to-vehicle transfer. 25 minutes from Beverly Hills, 30 minutes from Malibu.
  • Santa Monica Airport (KSMO): Closest to Santa Monica and Malibu. Excellent for beach-side arrivals with minimal transit time to Pacific Coast Highway destinations. Smaller ramp and limited availability during peak periods.
  • Burbank Airport (KBUR): Best for arrivals destined for Studio City, Burbank, or Pasadena. A quieter alternative to Van Nuys with good FBO access.
  • Long Beach Airport (KLGB): Ideal for clients staying in South Bay, Long Beach, or Orange County. Less traffic than Van Nuys with excellent FBO infrastructure.

Empty Leg Opportunities: Cabo to Los Angeles

Cabo to Los Angeles is one of the busiest private jet corridors in North America, which means it also generates more empty leg repositioning flights than almost any other short-haul route. An empty leg occurs when a private jet completes a one-way charter and must reposition to its home base — the operator sells those seats at a steep discount rather than fly empty.

  • Peak availability: December–April, when heavy inbound holiday and spring break traffic creates the most repositioning movements northbound to LA
  • Typical saving: $5,000–$15,000 off standard one-way charter rates
  • Available from as low as: $4,000–$6,000 one-way on a Light Jet (compared to $12,000+ standard)
  • Notice required: Typically 24–72 hours. Requires schedule flexibility but delivers exceptional value

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Cabo to Los Angeles private jet cost and charter logistics

How much does a Cabo to Los Angeles private jet cost?

The Cabo to Los Angeles private jet cost starts from $9,000–$14,000 one-way for a Very Light Jet, $12,000–$20,000 for a Light Jet, $18,000–$28,000 for a Midsize Jet, and $26,000–$38,000 for a Super-Midsize. Peak season pricing (December–April) runs 20–35% higher. For most groups of 4–8, the Light Jet category delivers the best balance of cost, comfort, and cabin experience on this 922-mile route. See our 2026 Cost Estimator for a precise quote.

How long is the flight from Cabo to Los Angeles by private jet?

The Cabo to LA private jet flight takes approximately 2 hours 25 minutes non-stop, covering 922 miles. All aircraft classes — from Very Light Jets to Heavy Jets — complete this route without a fuel stop. Total door-to-door time (including ground transport, private check-in, and terminal clearance at Van Nuys) is typically 3.5–4.5 hours depending on your LA destination.

Which airport do private jets use for Los Angeles from Cabo?

Van Nuys Airport (KVNY) is the primary arrival point for private jets from Cabo, offering dedicated FBO terminals, faster customs clearance, and zero commercial traffic. For clients heading to Santa Monica or Malibu, Santa Monica Airport (KSMO) saves 20–30 minutes of drive time. Burbank (KBUR) is best for Hollywood, Studio City, and Pasadena-based travellers.

What is the best private jet for Cabo to Los Angeles?

For most travellers, the Embraer Phenom 300 or Cessna Citation CJ3+ (Light Jet class) offers the ideal combination of cost, comfort, and cabin quality on this route. For groups of 8–10, the Citation XLS+ provides a full stand-up cabin at a reasonable midsize price point. A Heavy Jet is rarely necessary on a 922-mile route and adds significant cost without meaningful benefit.

Are there empty leg flights from Cabo to Los Angeles?

Yes — Cabo to LA is one of the most active empty leg corridors in North America. During peak season (December–April), empty leg Light Jets on this route can be secured from as little as $4,000–$6,000 one-way — 40–75% below standard charter rates.
