Private Jet vs Eurostar: Why Clients Choose to Fly

The Eurostar high-speed rail network connects Amsterdam Centraal directly to London St Pancras International in 3 hours and 52 minutes. While this is often marketed as a convenient center-to-center option, the realities of commercial passenger travel make it far less efficient for high-value executives and luxury travelers. The train journey time is only a fraction of the actual door-to-door transit time. Travelers must arrive at Amsterdam Centraal at least 30 to 45 minutes prior to departure for terminal check-in, baggage scanning, and international border security. Furthermore, transferring from St Pancras to final destinations in Mayfair, Belgravia, or the City of London typically adds 45 minutes to an hour of urban ground travel. The cumulative door-to-door journey time via Eurostar commonly exceeds 5 to 6 hours.

Choosing a private jet charter from Schiphol Jet Centre (EHAM) to London Farnborough (EGLF) or Biggin Hill (EGKB) reduces total door-to-door transit to under 2.5 hours. At Schiphol, executive terminal processing is completed in just 15 minutes, with pre-cleared international customs and secure tarmac parking. Once airborne, the non-stop flight takes only 56 minutes. Arriving at Farnborough puts passengers just 25 minutes from Mayfair via executive ground transfers, while Biggin Hill offers a 20-minute connection to Canary Wharf. Additionally, private jet passengers are free from public check-in halls and security queues, ensuring complete privacy, discretion, and confidentiality.

Furthermore, private aviation eliminates the rigid restrictions of commercial travel. Clients bring unlimited luggage, including heavy sports equipment, sensitive fine art, and pets, which are either restricted or completely prohibited on Eurostar. For corporate delegations or executive teams of four or more, the per-seat cost of a Light Jet (€4,500 ÷ 4 = €1,125 per seat) is highly competitive with Eurostar Business Premier tickets (which regularly retail between €350 and €600 each way), especially when the premium on executive time and in-flight productivity is considered. The Amsterdam–London corridor is also the busiest private aviation route in Northern Europe, creating a high volume of empty leg matching opportunities for discerning clients.

Which Aircraft Is Best for This Route?

For this short 212-mile hop across the English Channel, matching the correct aircraft category to your passenger group optimizes both comfort and flight economy. A Light Jet, such as the Embraer Phenom 300E or Cessna Citation CJ3+, is the premier choice for 1 to 5 passengers. These jets are highly fuel-efficient, capable of operating from shorter regional runways, and easily complete the flight in 56 minutes while offering comfortable club seating for up to 6–8 passengers. For larger groups of 6 to 9 passengers who require a stand-up cabin, a fully-equipped galley, and generous luggage capacity, a Midsize Jet like the Bombardier Challenger 350 or Embraer Praetor 500 is recommended. Heavy Jets, such as the Gulfstream G450 or Bombardier Global 5000, are ideal for large executive delegations, government summits, or VVIP clients who require a full cabin bedroom suite and premium flight attendant service. High-performance turboprops (e.g., King Air 350) can also complete this route at even lower cost for very small groups, offering a economical alternative with a minor 15-minute time addition.

Departure & Arrival Terminals

The efficiency of a private flight is heavily driven by the FBO and ground terminal logistics at each airport:

Amsterdam Departure: Schiphol Jet Centre (EHAM)

In Amsterdam, all private and business aviation flights depart from the Schiphol Jet Centre (EHAM), a dedicated executive terminal located on the east side of Amsterdam Airport Schiphol. Completely separate from the commercial passenger terminals, EHAM offers private customs clearance, luxury VIP lounges, and secure tarmac access for immediate vehicle boarding. The terminal boasts an average processing time of just 15 minutes. Schiphol Jet Centre records some of the highest private jet movements in continental Europe, serving as a primary link for global corporate leaders.

London Arrival Options

Depending on their final destination in the UK capital, clients select from several premier private airports:

  • Farnborough Airport (EGLF) London's premier private aviation hub, located 40 miles southwest of central London. Reserved exclusively for business aviation with no commercial traffic, it offers ultra-discrete handling and is only 25–35 minutes from Mayfair or Belgravia.
  • Biggin Hill Airport (EGKB) Located 14 miles southeast of the City of London, Biggin Hill is highly favored by financial sector clients and corporate teams, providing a fast 20-minute transfer to the City and Canary Wharf.
  • London Luton Airport (EGGW) An excellent option for clients continuing their travel north, or accessing Hertfordshire and the Home Counties directly.
  • RAF Northolt (EGWU) Available for diplomatic missions, military aircraft, and government delegations with prior official clearance.

European Event Calendar: When to Book

As the busiest private jet corridor in Northern Europe, the Amsterdam–London route experiences significant seasonal demand spikes driven by the European corporate, art, and sporting calendars:

  • January–March (Q1 Corporate Peak) Corporate budgeting, fiscal planning, and major financial summits in London and Amsterdam drive heavy corporate travel. Bookings should be secured 1–2 weeks in advance.
  • April (Masters & Monaco Pre-Season) Air traffic rises sharply as the European social season begins, with FIA F1 Grand Prix logistics causing slot congestion at Farnborough (EGLF).
  • May–June (Monaco GP, Cannes Film Festival, Royal Ascot) The single busiest travel window of the year for this corridor. FBO slots and aircraft are highly restricted; booking 4–6 weeks ahead is required to secure preferred aircraft, and a 20–30% price premium is common.
  • July–August (Wimbledon & Goodwood Festival of Speed) High demand from family travel and summer sporting events. We recommend booking flights at least 3 weeks in advance.
  • September (Amsterdam Art Fair & Frieze London) Peak season for art dealers, gallery owners, and financial elites. Early slots and aircraft procurement are highly advised.
  • October–December (ADE Amsterdam Dance Event & Holidays) Dance music executives and corporate clients fill slots in mid-October. Late December experiences peak holiday traffic; early booking is essential.

Empty Legs: Amsterdam to London at Discount

Because the Amsterdam to London corridor is the busiest business aviation route in Northern Europe, operators frequently execute one-way charters that require the aircraft to return to its home base or reposition for its next flight. These empty legs represent an excellent opportunity for flexible travelers, offering discounts of 40% to 70% off standard charter prices. The best empty leg availability occurs on same-day or next-day bookings when corporate jets reposition after corporate meetings in the Eurozone, or during peak event months (May–June and September) when high inbound charter volumes create outbound empty flights. Our concierge stays in constant contact with Middle East and European operators to match clients with live empty legs on the EHAM–EGLF and EHAM–EGKB corridors.
