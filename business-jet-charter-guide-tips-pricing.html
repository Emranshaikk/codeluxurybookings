What Is Business Jet Charter?

Business jet charter is the on-demand rental of a private aircraft for corporate or executive travel. Unlike fractional ownership or jet card memberships, chartering requires no long-term commitment. You pay per flight, gain full control over departure times, and enjoy a private cabin without shared passengers.

"Charter clients increasingly treat private aviation not as a luxury, but as a productivity tool — reclaiming hours that commercial travel would otherwise consume."

For organisations flying 10 to 400 hours per year, on-demand business jet charter often delivers better value than ownership. According to Aviation Week , the global business aviation market exceeded $32 billion in 2025 — with charter demand leading growth across EMEA and North America.

Types of Business Jet Charter Aircraft

Choosing the right aircraft is central to getting value from your business jet charter. The four main categories are:

Very Light Jets (VLJ)

Ideal for 2–4 passengers. Short hops up to 3 hours. Most affordable charter option.

Light Jets

4–6 seats. Range up to 2,000 nm. Perfect for intra-European or US domestic business trips.

Midsize Jets

6–8 passengers. Transcontinental range. The most popular segment for executive travel.

Heavy / Ultra-Long-Range Jets

8–19 seats. Non-stop intercontinental. Full cabin standing, bedroom suites, conference rooms.

For teams of 6–10, midsize jets like the Citation XLS+ or Hawker 900XP deliver the optimal balance of cabin space, range, and cost. You can explore detailed aircraft comparisons on JetSupport and Avinode's live marketplace .

Business Jet Charter Pricing Explained

The cost of a business jet charter depends on four key variables: aircraft category, route distance, positioning fees, and seasonal demand. Here's a practical pricing framework:

Aircraft Category Hourly Rate (Est.) Best For
Very Light Jet $2,500 – $4,000 Short hops, 1–3 passengers
Light Jet $4,000 – $6,500 Regional routes, 4–5 passengers
Midsize Jet $6,500 – $10,000 Executive travel, 6–8 passengers
Heavy Jet $10,000 – $18,000 Long-haul, groups of 8–14
Ultra-Long-Range $18,000 – $30,000+ Intercontinental, VIP travel

One powerful cost-saving strategy is booking empty leg flights — repositioning sectors where the aircraft flies without passengers. These can reduce your charter cost by 50–75%. Learn more on our ultimate guide to empty leg deals .

Pro tip: Always request an all-in quote that includes landing fees, fuel surcharges, and crew overnight costs — not just the hourly rate.

How to Book a Business Jet Charter (Step by Step)

Booking a business jet charter is simpler than most executives expect. Here's the process from enquiry to boarding:

  1. Define your mission: Date, departure airport, destination, passenger count, and any specific requirements such as WiFi, catering, or travelling with pets. If you travel with pets, see our pet-friendly private jet guide .
  2. Request a quote: Contact a reputable broker or operator with your mission details. The best charter companies will respond within 1–2 hours for standard routes.
  3. Review the charter agreement: Ensure the quote is all-inclusive, verify the operator holds an AOC (Air Operator Certificate), and check cancellation policies carefully.
  4. Confirm and fly: Most operators require a deposit of 25–50% on confirmation. Arrive 15 minutes before departure — no queues, no check-in desks.

7 Expert Tips for First-Time Business Jet Charter Buyers

  • Always use an ARGUS or Wyvern-rated operator. Safety ratings from these independent bodies are the gold standard for chartering a private jet safely.
  • Compare at least 3 quotes. Pricing for the same route can vary by 20–30% between operators. Use aggregators like PrivateFly alongside direct enquiries.
  • Consider empty legs for cost savings. If your schedule has flexibility, empty leg flights offer the same aircraft at dramatically reduced prices.
  • Book early for peak season. Summer in Europe (July–August) and US holiday weekends are the most constrained periods. Last-minute bookings during these windows can cost 40% more.
  • Know the difference between brokers and operators. Brokers source aircraft across fleets; operators own the aircraft. Both have advantages — brokers offer more choice, operators give more direct accountability.
  • Ask about in-flight WiFi and connectivity. For true business productivity, high-speed satellite WiFi is non-negotiable on flights over 2 hours.
  • Get everything in writing before you pay. Including the specific tail number, crew names, and handling agent at both airports. Verbal promises don't mean much at 06:00 on departure morning.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a business jet charter cost per hour?

Depending on aircraft size, business jet charter costs range from approximately $2,500/hour for a very light jet to $30,000+/hour for an ultra-long-range heavy jet. Most midsize executive charter flights fall in the $6,500–$10,000/hour range.

Can I charter a business jet for international flights?

Yes. Most charter operators handle international permits, customs, and overflight clearances. For intercontinental travel, heavy and ultra-long-range aircraft eliminate the need for fuel stops.

Is chartering a private jet cheaper than first class for groups?

For groups of 4 or more on time-sensitive routes, business jet charter often matches or beats the combined cost of first-class commercial tickets — while saving 3–5 hours per trip. According to NBAA research , the productivity value of time saved exceeds the price differential for most executives.

What is an empty leg flight?

An empty leg (also called a deadhead flight) occurs when a charter aircraft needs to reposition without passengers. These flights are sold at discounts of 50–75% off standard charter rates. See our empty leg deals page for current availability.
